FAQs

1. Location/directions:

Independent Lake Camp is in the beautiful Pocono Mountains in Orson, Pennsylvania, 45 minutes northeast of Scranton, PA and 45 minutes southeast of Binghamton, New York. The closest major airports are either NYC area airports or Philadelphia.

Although the camp is located in Orson Pennsylvania, the GPS address and summer postal address is Thompson, Pennsylvania. Orson is too small for its own post office. GPS address: 70 Clark Road, Thompson, PA 18465

2. What to pack:

Guests should bring their own bedding - either sleeping bags or twin sheets with pillows/blankets. Guests should also bring their own toiletries and towels for showering and the beach, as well as beach and outdoor-activity clothing.

For guests travelling from afar who have limited luggage space, please get in touch. The cabins are not heated, so please be sure to pack appropriate clothing and bedding as nights can be chilly, even in summer.

3. Weather:

Fingers crossed for warm weather, bright sunshine and a light refreshing breeze! But in all honesty this is the northeast and anything can happen.

Please pack layers, a raincoat, and sunscreen just in case!

4. Cabins:

The recently renovated camp cabins at ILC provide running water for showers, sinks and toilets, as well as single bed or double beds with mattresses.

The cabins are not heated and bedding and towels are not provided. Cabin arrangements range from roughly 10 people per cabin to private cabins and rooms with shared bathroom facilities. Bunk assignments will be made by Joel and Kelsey based on the overall needs of the guest list, but if you have specific concerns please get in touch.

5. Children:

While we love kids, the weekend is not designed with children in mind. If you have extenuating circumstances and would really love to attend but are unsure how to handle your childcare please get in touch.

6. Food:

As part of the cost of the full camp experience we will provide a BBQ dinner with beer and wine on Friday night, breakfast on Saturday and Sunday, and a sandwich lunch on Saturday.

All guests are invited to enjoy dinner and drinks as part of the ceremony and reception Saturday evening.

7. Dress Code:

Dress code for the weekend is generally casual. Layers are suggested for Friday evening’s BBQ as it can get chilly. Blankets will be provided for the bonfire.

Wedding ceremony and reception dress code is “summer soirée”. Anything from sundress to cocktail dress, and slacks and button down shirts. Jackets and ties optional. Shoes should be appropriate for walking and standing on grass.
